A Bachelor of Library and Information Science (BLIS) is an undergraduate academic degree program that prepares students for professional work in the field of library and information science. It focuses on the organization, storage, retrieval, and dissemination of information resources, as well as the management and administration of libraries and information centers. This degree equips students with the knowledge and skills to effectively organize and manage information, develop information systems, provide information services, and manage libraries and information centers. Graduates of BLIS programs can pursue careers as librarians, information specialists, information analysts, knowledge managers, and other related roles in various types of organizations.

A Bachelor of Science in Information Systems (BSIS) is an undergraduate degree program that focuses on the intersection of technology, business, and people. It covers the design, development, and management of information systems that support business operations and decision-making processes. This degree program includes courses on database management, programming, systems analysis and design, project management, and business fundamentals. Graduates of BSIS programs are equipped with the skills to design and implement information systems that meet the needs of businesses and organizations. They can pursue careers as business analysts, systems analysts, database administrators, project managers, and other related roles in various industries.
